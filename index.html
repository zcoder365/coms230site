Equality

Created by the Foundationals, our government is structured so that everyone is equal. They believed in true equality and balance, hence outlining the values below.


The Parties

Today, there are 5 parties that each have their own unique personalities. These parties each have a representative for citizens to vote for. The five parties are Red, Yellow, Green, Blue, and Purple. Read the section titled "The Parties" below to learn more about each of their beliefs.


Civic Engagement

Civic engagement is important to the government. Citizens can vote in local elections at age 16. At age 18, citizens gain access to voting in the federal election. Further, if employed, you are given time off to be encouraged to vote. Otherwise, you will get fined.


Education

Since civic engagement is important, it is mandated that all schools teach their students about the government. This page is a good place to start your research!


The Government's Structure

President

The roles that the president has including being the head of state and head of government. Further, the president has authority over the state and the army.

The president also has the tie breaker vote when there is a 50/50 tie. Further, the president represents the country when they travel internationally.

The president has power over choosing their secretaries, nominating constitutional judges, and rank choice voting. The presiden't, however, does not have veto power.


Vice President

The vice president (VP) has a symbolic role. They will act on the global scale when the president is not available, and is chosen by the president. They run together, so if citizens vote for the president, they run for the vice president too. In the case of the president being impeached, the VP will replace the president.


Constitutional Court

The members of the Consitutional Court (CC) are nominated by the president and confirmed by the legislative council (read more below). This council is five members. To be a member of the CC, the nominees need to have a legal background (i.e. being a lawyer). Lastly, the CC has the ability of blocking unconstitutional laws.


Legislative Council

In the Legislative Council (LC), there are 100 members. This is the 1% threshold to approve secretaries. If there is a tie (50%-50%), then there has to be one person that breaks the tie. The LC can appoint CC judges. The LC can also vote for the budget and vote to remove the president if they feel the president is unfit. Members can propose bills.

Aside from the general members of the LC, there is a speaker of the LC, who is appointed by the president. The speaker is not a member of the LC; their main role is to regulate the debates.


Municipalities

The municipalities include the mayor and municipal councils. In the municipal council, every two years in November, deputies are elected to represent each district. If there are under 500,000 civilians, they are divided into 10 districts. If there are over 500,000 citizens, they divide into 50 districts. This division allows for municipalities to have deputies that will hear and understand the local problems of the city.

The Government's Values

Our government's values are...

  • equality (more specifically, equal representation)
  • inclusivity
  • rule of law
  • transparency and honesty
  • welfare
  • free media
  • freedom assembly and freedom of speech
  • civic engagement

What the government means by equality is equal representation. They want all communities, socio-economic groups, races, and ethnicities to be represented. This relates to inclusivity as all groups's opinions should be represented and considered when creating laws. Further, it is important for all citizens to follow the laws. This is what is meant by rule of law.

The government additionally believes in transparency and honesty. This includes the government sharing updates about legislature. The government also cares about welfare. In other words, they will give aid to those who need support.

The government believes that its citizens should have freedom of assembly and freedom of speech.
